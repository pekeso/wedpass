# WedPass Beta Plan

## 1. Document Purpose

This document defines the beta launch plan for **WedPass V1.0**.

WedPass V1.0 will launch as a free beta to validate whether the platform works in real wedding conditions.

The beta plan covers:

- Beta goals
- Target beta users
- Wedding selection criteria
- Onboarding process
- Event-day preparation
- Support plan
- Success metrics
- Feedback collection
- Post-beta analysis
- Decision criteria for V1.1

---

## 2. Beta Strategy

WedPass should not start with a wide public launch.

The first beta should be:

```text
Small
Controlled
Hands-on
High-learning
```

Recommended first beta size:

```text
3 to 5 weddings
```

This is enough to test the core product without creating too much support pressure.

---

## 3. Beta Objective

The main beta objective is:

> Prove that WedPass can reliably support real wedding guest check-in and media collection under real African event conditions.

The beta is not primarily about revenue.

The beta is about validating:

- Offline check-in reliability
- Staff usability
- Organizer setup clarity
- Guest media participation
- Media upload reliability
- Overall product usefulness
- Operational support needs

---

## 4. Beta Positioning

Position WedPass beta as:

```text
A free early-access wedding guest check-in and photo/video collection platform.
```

Recommended beta message:

> WedPass helps you manage wedding guest entry and collect photos/videos from your guests in one place, even when internet at the venue is unreliable.

Avoid positioning it as:

- A full wedding planning suite
- A wedding website builder
- A social media platform
- A vendor marketplace

---

## 5. Target Beta Users

Ideal beta users:

- Couples planning a wedding soon
- Wedding planners
- Event coordinators
- Family members managing guest entry
- Churches or community groups helping organize weddings

Target regions:

- Central Africa
- East Africa
- West Africa
- English-speaking and French-speaking markets

Recommended first beta users:

- People you can communicate with directly
- People willing to give feedback
- Weddings where you can test before event day
- Organizers who understand this is beta software

---

## 6. Wedding Selection Criteria

## 6.1 Ideal Beta Wedding

An ideal beta wedding has:

- 100 to 500 guests
- 1 to 3 entrance/check-in points
- 2 to 4 staff devices
- Willing organizer
- Some expected internet unreliability
- Guests likely to take photos/videos
- Organizer willing to test before event day

---

## 6.2 Avoid for First Beta

Avoid weddings that are:

- Too large, such as 1,000+ guests
- Extremely high-pressure VIP events
- Events where failure would create major reputational damage
- Events with no pre-event testing opportunity
- Events with organizers who expect a polished paid product
- Events requiring RSVP/invitation builder/seating chart

These can come later after V1.0 is proven.

---

## 7. Beta Offer

Recommended first beta offer:

```text
Free use of WedPass for your wedding in exchange for structured feedback.
```

What beta users get:

- Free wedding setup
- Guest list import
- QR code generation
- Offline staff check-in
- Photo/video upload page
- Gallery access
- Post-event media access
- Direct support

What WedPass gets:

- Real-world usage
- Feedback
- Bug reports
- Product validation
- Testimonials if successful
- Usage metrics

---

## 8. Beta User Commitments

Beta users should agree to:

- Provide guest list before event day
- Test staff devices before event day
- Allow WedPass to collect product feedback
- Report issues honestly
- Complete post-event feedback form
- Accept that the product is in beta

Optional:

- Provide testimonial if satisfied
- Allow anonymized case study

---

## 9. Beta Onboarding Flow

## 9.1 Step 1: Initial Conversation

Goal:

- Understand wedding context
- Confirm fit for beta

Questions:

- Wedding date?
- Country/city?
- Estimated guest count?
- Number of entrances?
- Who will manage check-in?
- Expected internet quality at venue?
- Do guests usually use WhatsApp heavily?
- Do you want photo/video collection?
- Are you willing to test before event day?

---

## 9.2 Step 2: Create Wedding in WedPass

Organizer creates or WedPass team helps create:

- Wedding name
- Couple names
- Wedding date
- Location
- Country
- Cover image if available

---

## 9.3 Step 3: Import Guest List

Supported options:

- Organizer uploads CSV
- WedPass manually helps format CSV during beta
- Small weddings may add guests manually

Guest list fields:

- Full name
- Phone number optional
- Email optional
- Allowed guest count

Important:

- Keep guest data simple.
- Avoid unnecessary fields in V1.0.

---

## 9.4 Step 4: Generate QR Codes

After guest import:

- Generate QR code per guest
- Export/download QR codes
- Decide how QR codes will be shared/printed

Possible QR usage:

- Printed at reception desk
- Sent to guests manually
- Embedded in simple invitation message
- Used by staff from guest list

V1.0 does not include a full invitation builder.

---

## 9.5 Step 5: Staff Device Preparation

Before wedding day:

- Create staff devices
- Give staff access
- Download offline pack on each device
- Test QR scanning
- Test manual search
- Test offline check-in
- Test sync after reconnecting

Every staff device must show:

```text
Offline Pack Ready
```

before the event.

---

## 9.6 Step 6: Guest Media Link Preparation

Prepare:

- Guest upload link
- QR poster for upload page
- Short instruction message
- MC announcement text

Example message:

```text
Share your photos and short videos from the wedding here: [WedPass link]
```

Example MC announcement:

```text
Please scan the QR code on your table or at the entrance to share your photos and videos with the couple through WedPass.
```

---

## 10. Event-Day Checklist

Before guests arrive:

- [ ] Staff devices charged
- [ ] Staff devices have internet tested
- [ ] Offline pack downloaded on every device
- [ ] QR scanner tested
- [ ] Manual search tested
- [ ] Sync status understood by staff
- [ ] Backup power bank available
- [ ] Guest upload QR displayed
- [ ] Organizer knows where to check stats
- [ ] Staff know what to do if offline

During check-in:

- [ ] Staff use QR scan first
- [ ] Staff use manual search if QR fails
- [ ] Staff do not panic if offline
- [ ] Staff monitor pending sync count
- [ ] Staff sync when internet returns

After check-in:

- [ ] Every staff device opens Sync Status
- [ ] Pending sync count becomes zero
- [ ] Organizer checks final check-in count
- [ ] Staff devices are not cleared until synced

---

## 11. Staff Training Script

Use simple language.

Training points:

1. This app works even if internet disappears.
2. Always check the top sync bar.
3. If offline, keep checking guests in.
4. The app saves check-ins on the device.
5. Use QR scan first.
6. If QR fails, search by name or phone.
7. After the rush, press Sync Now when internet is available.
8. Do not clear local data unless told to.

Short version:

```text
Scan, confirm, check in. If internet fails, continue. The phone saves everything and syncs later.
```

---

## 12. Guest Media Participation Strategy

Guests may naturally prefer WhatsApp, so WedPass must make upload participation easy and visible.

Recommended actions:

- Display QR code at entrance
- Display QR code near tables
- Ask MC to announce upload page
- Ask couple/planner to share link in WhatsApp groups
- Keep upload page simple
- Allow post-event uploads
- Use warm message: “Share your favorite moments with the couple”

## 14. Beta Success Criteria

WedPass V1.0 beta is successful if:

- 3 to 5 weddings use the platform
- No check-in data is lost
- Offline check-in works in real conditions
- Staff can use the app without developer intervention
- Sync succeeds after internet returns
- At least 90% of upload attempts succeed or queue safely
- Organizers understand Event Mode setup
- At least 20 structured feedback points are collected
- At least one organizer would recommend using it again

---

## 15. Beta Failure Criteria

Beta signals serious problems if:

- Check-in data is lost
- Staff cannot check in guests offline
- Sync creates incorrect check-in counts
- Staff cannot understand the interface
- Organizer setup is too difficult
- Uploads fail most of the time
- Guest media participation is near zero
- Security/privacy issue occurs

If these happen, pause broader beta and fix before adding more weddings.

---

## 16. Feedback Collection

## 16.1 Organizer Feedback Questions

Ask:

1. Was setup easy?
2. Was guest import clear?
3. Was Event Mode preparation clear?
4. Did check-in work as expected?
5. Did staff understand the app?
6. Did guests upload photos/videos?
7. What confused you?
8. What feature did you expect but did not see?
9. Would you use WedPass again?
10. Would you pay for this later?

---

## 16.2 Staff Feedback Questions

Ask:

1. Was QR scanning easy?
2. Was manual search easy?
3. Did you understand offline mode?
4. Did you trust that check-ins were saved?
5. Was anything too small or confusing?
6. What slowed you down?

---

## 16.3 Guest Feedback Questions

Ask:

1. Was upload easy?
2. Did the page load fast enough?
3. Did you understand what to do?
4. Did you prefer sending photos via WhatsApp?
5. What would make you upload more?

---

## 17. Post-Wedding Review Process

After each beta wedding:

1. Confirm all devices synced.
2. Export or review check-in totals.
3. Review duplicate conflicts.
4. Review sync logs.
5. Review media upload success/failure.
6. Review guest participation.
7. Collect organizer feedback.
8. Collect staff feedback if possible.
9. Document bugs.
10. Update risk register.
11. Update backlog.
12. Decide whether to proceed to next beta wedding.

---

## 18. Beta Support Plan

For first beta weddings, support should be hands-on.

Before event:

- Help organizer import guests if needed.
- Verify staff devices.
- Confirm offline pack readiness.
- Share quick staff instructions.

During event:

- Be reachable if possible.
- Avoid making live changes unless critical.
- Encourage staff to keep checking in offline if network fails.

After event:

- Confirm sync completion.
- Help organizer access media.
- Collect feedback.

---

## 19. Beta Communication Templates

## 19.1 Invitation to Beta User

```text
Hi [Name],

We are testing WedPass, a simple wedding guest check-in and photo/video collection platform.

It helps you manage your guest list, check guests in using QR codes, and collect photos/videos from guests in one place. It is designed to keep check-in working even if internet at the venue is unreliable.

We are offering free beta access in exchange for honest feedback after the event.

Would you be open to testing it for your wedding?
```

---

## 19.2 Staff Instruction Message

```text
You will use WedPass to check in guests at the wedding.

Before guests arrive, make sure the offline pack is downloaded on your phone.

During check-in:
1. Scan the guest QR code.
2. Confirm the guest name.
3. Tap Check In.
4. If QR scanning fails, search the guest manually.
5. If internet goes off, continue checking guests in. The app saves check-ins on the phone and syncs later.
```

---

## 19.3 Guest Upload Message

```text
Help us collect beautiful memories from the wedding.

Please upload your favorite photos and short videos here:
[WedPass link]

Thank you for sharing these moments with the couple.
```

---

## 20. Beta Operational Limits

For V1.0 beta, limit usage to avoid overload.

Recommended limits:

```text
Weddings: 3–5 initially
Guests per wedding: ideally 100–500
Staff devices: 2–5
Image upload max: 10MB
Video upload max: 100MB
Media retention: during beta unless deletion requested
```

Avoid:

- Public self-serve launch
- Very large weddings
- Unlimited video uploads
- Weddings with no preparation time

---

## 21. Data and Privacy During Beta

Rules:

- Tell organizers what data is collected.
- Do not expose guest lists publicly.
- Do not use real guest data in demos without permission.
- Delete data if organizer requests it.
- Do not publish wedding media without permission.
- Ask permission before using testimonials or screenshots.

---

## 22. Beta Learnings to Capture

After beta, answer:

- Is offline check-in valuable enough?
- Who is the real buyer: couple, planner, venue, family, church?
- Is guest media upload compelling?
- Do users need RSVP next?
- Do users need invitation builder next?
- Are people willing to pay?
- What price range feels acceptable?
- Which features caused confusion?
- Which features were not used?
- What should be V1.1?

---

## 23. V1.1 Decision Criteria

Only move to V1.1 after answering:

1. Did V1.0 work reliably?
2. Did users understand the core product?
3. Did organizers want to use it again?
4. Did guests upload media?
5. Did staff trust offline check-in?
6. What feature was requested most?
7. What would users pay for?

Potential V1.1 features:

- CSV export
- Multi-wedding accounts
- Basic branding customization
- Media ZIP download
- Storage limits
- Light polling dashboard
- Public guest upload link improvements
- Basic paid plans

---

## 24. Beta Anti-Patterns

Do not:

- Open public signup too early
- Add RSVP before validating check-in/media
- Add AI features before core reliability
- Use untested staff devices at a real wedding
- Skip offline pack preparation
- Ignore staff feedback
- Ignore low upload participation
- Treat beta like a finished product
- Overpromise features not in V1.0

---

## 25. Summary

WedPass beta should be controlled, hands-on, and learning-focused.

The main beta question is:

> Can WedPass reliably manage wedding guest check-in and collect wedding memories in real African event conditions?

If the answer is yes, then V1.1 can expand carefully.

If the answer is no, the next step is not more features. The next step is fixing reliability and usability.
